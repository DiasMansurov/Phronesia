# Phronesia Pilot Launch Playbook

## Goal

Recruit the first 10 to 15 economics teachers who will:

- try Phronesia in class or as homework
- give feedback on what confused students
- tell us whether they would use a more polished classroom pack

The first launch is not about scale. It is about getting believable teacher signal fast.

## Core Positioning

Use this sentence everywhere:

> Phronesia is a macroeconomics simulation where students run the economy, make fiscal and monetary policy decisions, and deal with inflation, unemployment, approval, elections, and political backlash in real time.

Teacher-specific angle:

> I am an economics teacher and I built it because I needed a better way to engage my own students with macro trade-offs.

Founder follow-up:

> Now I am sharing it with other teachers in the community and looking for honest feedback on whether it works in real classrooms.

Pilot ask:

> I am looking for a small group of economics teachers willing to try one short run and tell me what worked, what confused students, and what would make it classroom-ready.

## Best First Channels

These are the highest-leverage places to start before paid acquisition.

1. Teacher communities where economics educators already gather
2. Warm community posts in subject-specific spaces
3. Small-volume direct outreach to teachers with public contact pages
4. LinkedIn founder posts
5. A very small incentive budget for pilot credits, not ads

Relevant teacher ecosystems to target:

- AP Community / AP Central for AP educators: [AP Community](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/professional-learning/ap-community)
- Council for Economic Education teacher network: [CEE for Teachers](https://www.councilforeconed.org/programs/for-teachers/)
- Foundation for Teaching Economics teacher network: [FTE Teachers](https://fte.org/teachers/)
- National Association of Economic Educators: [NAEE](https://www.naee.net/)
- MRU economics teacher Facebook ecosystem: [MRU Facebook links](https://mru.org/fb)

## Recommended Budget

Do not spend the first $100 on ads.

Recommended allocation:

- $80: app credits or future teacher-pack credits for early pilot teachers who give substantive feedback
- $20: contingency for a one-pager, small creative asset need, or a niche boosted post if a group specifically allows it

## Launch Sequence

### Phase 1: teacher pilot recruitment

Objective: recruit the first 10 teachers

Channels:

- 2 to 4 Reddit posts in non-spammy education/econ-adjacent subreddits
- 3 to 5 teacher community posts where allowed
- 20 to 30 direct emails to public teacher contacts over one week

Success metric:

- 10 teacher signups or replies
- 5 actual classroom trials or review calls

### Phase 2: student-facing proof

Only after teacher signal:

- collect 3 to 5 teacher quotes
- collect screenshots or classroom use stories
- then post more broadly to student and education audiences

## Immediate Risks

These do not block outreach, but they will limit conversion:

1. The site still looks like a polished project, not yet a validated classroom tool with proof
2. The teacher waitlist page is not yet framed as a time-bound pilot cohort
3. There is no explicit incentive or clear expected time commitment for teachers

## Site Changes To Make Next

These should be the next product-side adjustments before a bigger push:

- add a clear “Join the pilot cohort” message on the teacher page
- say exactly what the teacher gets: early access, classroom pack input, short feedback call
- say exactly what the teacher is being asked to do: 1 short run plus 10 to 20 minutes of feedback
- add a founder note to make the ask human

## Pilot Offer

Use this offer consistently:

> Looking for 10 economics teachers to test Phronesia. Try one short macro simulation with your class or on your own, then send feedback. Early pilot teachers will shape the classroom version and receive app credits or future teacher-pack credit for helping with the pilot.

## Reddit Strategy

Do not start with aggressive self-promotion.

Best approach:

- choose small number of relevant communities
- read rules first
- frame the post as “looking for teacher feedback” rather than “buy my product”
- include one sentence about what students actually do
- ask a concrete question to invite comments

Candidate subreddit types:

- teacher communities
- teaching resources communities
- economics education adjacent communities
- IB/AP student communities later, not first

## Teacher Interview Questions

Use these when someone agrees to talk:

1. Where would this fit most naturally in your course?
2. What would students understand immediately?
3. What would they need explained before using it?
4. Would you use it as a lesson opener, homework task, revision activity, or competition?
5. What would make this feel classroom-ready?
6. Would you want a teacher dashboard, worksheet, debrief questions, or lesson plan first?
